RubixKube is autonomous, not reckless. The system can recommend and apply changes at machine speed, but every mutation passes through policies you control. This page is the plain-English model of how the safety layer works.

The single most important thing to know: **RubixKube does not touch your infrastructure without an explicit approval.** The Observer is read-only. Anything that could change state goes through the Guardian Agent and a policy you configured.

## The three layers

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Read-only by default" icon="lock">
    The Observer has read-only scopes on Kubernetes, AWS, GCP, and VMs. No mutating permissions are provisioned when you install.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Policy at the Guardian" icon="shield-halved">
    Every Action runs through Guardian policy for the target environment. The policy decides who approves, how many approvers, and which scopes are forbidden.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Audit on everything" icon="file-lines">
    Every Action, approval, and outcome is logged with actor and timestamp. Rollback is one click. Audit is immutable.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What the Guardian Agent does

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Validates scope" icon="box">
    Every Action names the resources it touches. The Guardian refuses anything outside the environment's allowed scope.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Checks blast radius" icon="explosion">
    If the Action's computed blast radius exceeds the policy's threshold, the Guardian requires extra approvers or outright blocks the Action.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Enforces approvers" icon="user-check">
    How many approvers, who can approve, and whether the approver can be the person who proposed the Action. All configurable per environment.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Gates destructive operations" icon="skull-crossbones">
    Delete, wipe, force-restart, secret rotation, and similar operations always require named approvers, regardless of environment.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Serialises conflicts" icon="arrow-right-arrow-left">
    If two Actions would touch the same resource at the same time, the Guardian orders them. No interleaving, no race conditions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Policy knobs, in plain language

Guardian policies are not abstract. They are concrete knobs per environment.

| Knob | What it controls |
| --- | --- |
| **Approver count** | How many humans must approve before Execute |
| **Approver set** | Which users, teams, or roles can approve |
| **Self-approval** | Whether the proposer can also be an approver |
| **Blast-radius cap** | Maximum allowed blast radius in number of dependent resources |
| **Destructive operations** | Explicit allow/block list for delete, wipe, force operations |
| **Auto-approve scope** | Which specific low-risk Actions can run without approval (opt-in) |
| **Quiet hours** | Windows when approvals route to a different on-call group |
| **Read-only mode** | Hard lock that disables all Execute paths for the environment |

Defaults vary by environment criticality ([Environments](/concepts/environments)). Lab is permissive, production is strict.

## The approval flow, end to end

<Steps titleSize="h3">
  <Step title="Action proposed">
    An RCA, a skill, or a chat thread produces an Action. It appears in the Action Center and in the configured notification channels.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Guardian validates">
    Scope, blast radius, destructive content, and policy eligibility all checked. If any check fails, the Action moves to Blocked or Needs Extra Approvers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Human reviews">
    The approver sees the scope, the change payload, the blast radius, the confidence score, and the rollback plan. If anything is wrong, they reject with a reason.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply">
    Approved Actions run inside their scoped blast radius. The Remediation Agent handles the mechanics.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify and audit">
    Stabilisation window confirms the outcome. The audit record captures everything.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What never happens

- **RubixKube does not delete data without explicit named approvers and a confirmed backup reference.**
- **RubixKube does not rotate secrets silently.** Every rotation produces an Action with the scope visible.
- **RubixKube does not touch resources outside the environment's declared scope.**
- **RubixKube does not apply two conflicting Actions in parallel.** They are serialised.
- **RubixKube does not hide audit records.** Immutable, exportable, retained per plan.

## The quotable one-liner

From the pricing FAQ on [rubixkube.ai/pricing](https://rubixkube.ai/pricing):

> Does RubixKube touch my infrastructure? No. We watch, we analyse, we recommend. Your team decides what to do. Nothing changes without you.

That is the default. Policies let you loosen it (for lab environments) or tighten it (for regulated production) exactly as much as your team wants.

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I set different policies per environment?">
    Yes. Every environment has its own Guardian policy. Production can require two approvers, staging can require one, lab can auto-approve low-risk changes. See [Environments](/concepts/environments).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I block an entire category of actions?">
    Yes. The destructive operations list is configurable. If you want to disallow pod deletion, IAM changes, or secret rotation entirely in a given environment, a one-line policy does it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What about RBAC for approvers?">
    Enterprise workspaces integrate with SSO (SAML, OIDC). Approvers map to roles from your identity provider. Lower tiers use workspace-level roles.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is the Guardian itself auditable?">
    Yes. Every Guardian decision (approve, reject, require-extra, block) has an audit entry. Policies themselves are versioned and exportable.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if an urgent action cannot wait for approval?">
    Break-glass is supported on Enterprise. A named user can bypass standard approval with a signed justification, logged as such. The audit record preserves the break-glass flag forever.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does Guardian handle edge cases the policy did not anticipate?">
    Deny by default. If a scope, blast radius, or operation type is not explicitly allowed, the Guardian refuses and asks for human review. Safe defaults over clever defaults.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
